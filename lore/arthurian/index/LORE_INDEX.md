# Arthurian Lore Index

This is the first working lore index for Eldermere. It is intentionally source-facing: it identifies what the game must cover before large original Arthurian expansion.

## Core Figures

- Arthur: birth, hidden upbringing, sword test, coronation, wars of consolidation, Roman campaign traditions, Round Table kingship, final conflict, death or removal to Avalon.
- Merlin: prophetic counselor, engineer of Arthur's birth and kingship, strategist, enchanter, and source of ambiguous fate.
- Uther Pendragon and Igraine: parentage layer behind Arthur's legitimacy and hidden birth.
- Guinevere/Gwenhwyvar: queen figure with differing Welsh, romance, Malory, and Tennyson emphases.
- Lancelot: central romance and downfall figure in later tradition, especially Malory and Tennyson.
- Gawain/Gawaine/Gwalchmai: major knight across romance and Welsh material; useful for honor, kinship, vengeance, and courtly adventure arcs.
- Mordred/Modred: final betrayal and succession-conflict figure; spelling and emphasis vary by source.
- Morgan le Fay: Arthur's kin, enchantress, adversarial and ambiguous figure; tied to Accolon, scabbard theft, and magical intervention in Malory.
- Galahad, Percival/Peredur, Bors: Grail-quest axis, with Welsh and romance variants.
- Kay/Kai: Arthur's seneschal in Welsh and romance materials; tone varies from heroic to sharp-tongued or comic.
- Bedivere: final-sword/last-loyalist role in later Arthurian death traditions.

## Relics And Objects

- Sword in the Stone: legitimacy test and public proof of kingship.
- Excalibur/Caliburn: royal sword traditions vary across sources; distinguish sword acquisition from later scabbard and return/death motifs.
- Excalibur's scabbard: in Malory, a separate protective object with major plot importance.
- Round Table: political and chivalric institution, wedding gift/formation in Malory, broader symbol in later retellings.
- Holy Grail: spiritual quest center, especially important before large late-game story expansion.
- Questing Beast: strange dynastic/quest omen linked to Pellinore and later quest material.

## Places

- Camelot: court center in later romance; game hub for politics, underworld, and faction play.
- Caerleon/Carlion: important Welsh and romance court setting.
- Avalon: death/healing/otherworld endpoint and route concept.
- Logres/Britain: Arthur's political-mythic realm.
- Tintagel: useful for Uther/Igraine/Arthur birth traditions.
- Rome: imperial conflict layer, especially Geoffrey/Malory traditions.
- Underworld/Otherworld crossings: not strictly one Arthurian place, but useful connective tissue for later Greek/Celtic interaction packs.

## Source Variation Points

- Arthur's earliest historical traces differ from later romance kingship.
- Welsh material gives different names, tones, and court dynamics from Malory/Tennyson.
- Geoffrey gives a pseudo-historical imperial Arthur; Malory gives a chivalric romance synthesis; Tennyson gives a Victorian moral-tragic arc.
- Excalibur, Caliburn, Sword in the Stone, and scabbard traditions should be tracked separately.
- Mordred/Modred and Guinevere/Gwenhwyvar vary by source and language tradition.
- Avalon can support probabilistic endings, dream routes, healing, exile, or return-prophecy variants.

## Game Expansion Rules

- First cover the major lore beats above.
- Then add original Eldermere connective tissue: under-market factions, companions, relic variants, dice outcomes, and cross-myth interactions.
- Every original main-story arc should cite which lore beat it grows from.
- Every major lore contradiction should become a possible branch, rumor, faction claim, or probabilistic event.
