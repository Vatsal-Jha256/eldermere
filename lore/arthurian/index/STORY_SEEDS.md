# Arthurian Story Seeds

These are original Eldermere seeds derived from the lore index. They are not source text.

## Main Arc Spine

1. The Sword Test: players begin in a socially messy Camelot where legitimacy is still argued in taverns, markets, churches, and under-bridges.
2. Merlin's Ledger: Merlin's prophecies exist as conflicting debts, maps, and warnings rather than a clean quest log.
3. The Scabbard Problem: Excalibur is not only a weapon; the missing scabbard becomes a systems hook for survival, curses, and faction politics.
4. Round Table Fractures: knights are not only bosses or allies; they are faction anchors whose reputations shift with player action.
5. Grail Sorting: the Grail quest changes what kinds of companions, relics, and oaths can follow the player.
6. Mordred's Claim: Mordred's faction uses legal, prophetic, and underworld arguments, not only villain force.
7. Avalon Ambiguity: the endgame should not immediately answer whether Arthur dies, heals, returns, or becomes a route between legends.

## Side Arc Families

- Under-market relic trade: Excalibur fragments, false scabbards, oath tokens, saint bones, and foreign bronze.
- Welsh court echoes: Kai, Owain, Peredur, Geraint, and Gwenhwyvar variants appear as alternate rumors and quests.
- Morgan's bargains: magical protection always costs a political or personal debt.
- Gawain's kinship web: family loyalty can override court ideals.
- Grail witnesses: different companions interpret the same miracle differently.
- Cross-myth leaks: Greek oracles, Celtic otherworld routes, and later legend packs react to Arthurian tags.

## Added Expansion Seeds

- Tintagel birth ledger: Uther, Igraine, Merlin, secrecy, legitimacy, and parentage become playable evidence rather than a static prologue.
- Guinevere/Lancelot pressure: courtly love, surveillance, loyalty, and rumor begin as social pressure before they become a fall-of-Camelot disaster.
- Kay's hard office: Kai/Kay's older and later tones become a court-logistics arc about keys, food, insults, and discipline.
- Bedivere's last witness: the sword-return and last-loyalist motifs become an ending-preparation arc before Avalon.
