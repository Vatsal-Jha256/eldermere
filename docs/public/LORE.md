# Lore

## Lore Model

Eldermere treats Arthurian material as source-facing rather than adaptation-first.

- Public-domain legend and source texts are the reference layer.
- Each original story arc should grow from a named lore beat.
- Contradictions are not cleaned away. They become branches, rumors, faction claims, or alternate quests.
- Cross-myth material is allowed when it creates usable connective tissue between packs.

The source index lives in `lore/arthurian/index/LORE_INDEX.md`.

## Core Subjects

The lore index currently centers these Arthurian figures and objects:

- Arthur, Merlin, Uther, Igraine
- Guinevere, Lancelot, Gawain, Mordred, Morgan, Galahad, Percival, Bors, Kay, Bedivere
- Sword in the Stone, Excalibur, the scabbard, the Round Table, the Grail, the Questing Beast
- Camelot, Caerleon, Avalon, Tintagel, Rome, Britain, and otherworld crossings

## Current Coverage

### Arthurian Core

The main spine now covers:

- Sword test and contested kingship
- Merlin's ledger, prophecy, and debt logic
- Excalibur and the missing scabbard as separate problems
- Round Table fracture and reputation pressure
- Grail sorting and witness logic
- Mordred's claim and succession conflict
- Avalon ambiguity as an ending rather than a fixed answer
- Morgan, Gawain, Tintagel, Guinevere/Lancelot pressure, Kay, and Bedivere side arcs

### Camelot Underbelly

This pack carries the starter quest and the under-market relic economy.

- Lantern Yard
- Tavern Backroom
- Market Under
- River Stairs
- Smuggler Vault

Its job is to make the Arthurian court feel economically and politically lived-in before the myth gets bigger.

### Greek Crossing

This pack introduces cross-legend routes and underworld logic.

- Oracle and ferry routes
- Underworld leakage
- Cross-myth reactions to Arthurian tags such as `excalibur-rumor` and `grail-curse`

It is the first visible example of the shared-world rule: one myth pack can react to another without becoming a separate game.

## Expansion Gaps

The lore index still marks several families as thin or missing:

- Early wars against rival kings
- Roman campaign traditions
- Caerleon as a court hub
- Pellinore and the Questing Beast
- Gareth, Tristram, Bors, and Accolon-centered arcs
- A fuller Camlann sequence that ties the endings together

Those gaps are useful as a roadmap for future packs.

## Writing Rules

- Write original prose.
- Use public-domain source material as grounding.
- Do not copy modern adaptation dialogue, scenes, or unique invention.
- Prefer playable connections: tags, debts, relics, roads, witnesses, omens, factions, and consequences.
